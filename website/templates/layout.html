An Analysis of the pain pill Crisis

Opioid Use and Related Mortality in the United States: 2006-2012

Is there a correlation between legal opioid prescriptions and opioid overdoses in the US?

In 2018, the CDC lowered its life expectancy of Americans for the third time since 2014, the first lowering of life expectancy for Americans since World War 2 in 1944. Drug overdoses and suicide were cited among the reasons for this decline in life expectancy, with opioid related overdose deaths the largest contributing factor to mortality.

Aggressive marketing for prescription pain killers and increases in opioid prescriptions for pain management and abuse of these prescriptions created the current opioid epidemic, a major public health crisis in the United States of America (USA)[1,2]. In 2014, 61% of 47,055 total drug overdose deaths involved opioids[3].

After winning a lawsuit and releasing the ARCOS dataset, analyzed below, in August 2019, The Washington Post encouraged others to take the data and do further analysis on it. Our project sought to take the analysis done by The Washington Post and add an additional layer of analysis. By running a geographically weighted regression on the CDC Mortality data merged with the ARCOS dataset, we sought to answer the question Is there a correlation between legal opioid prescriptions and opioid overdoses in the US?
